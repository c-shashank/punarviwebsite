About Us

A family’s love, bottled as Ayurveda.

Long before there was a brand called Punarvi, there was a woman standing in a sunlit kitchen, grinding herbs the way her grandmother once did. She was the friend who always arrived with a little jar of something—an ubtan that calmed angry skin, an oil that soothed a tired scalp, a paste that smelled like temple flowers and monsoon earth. What began as care for her own sensitive skin slowly became a quiet obsession: to prove that Ayurveda, in its purest form, could still belong on our bathroom shelves.

She held a demanding day job, but her evenings were for dalas and mortars, for scribbled notes and family recipes. Batch after batch, she refused shortcuts. No ready-made bases, no synthetic stabilisers, no “just a little” fragrance to make things smell more marketable. Each failure stung, but every small success—a rash that calmed, a scar that softened—felt like a blessing.

Founder portrait placeholder

Through all of this, her husband watched her eyes light up in ways that spreadsheets never did. He was the first to offer his own hair and skin as test ground, the one who brewed her late-night chai when a batch split at 2 a.m. While she perfected ratios of manjistha and nalpamaradi, he read up on sourcing, packaging, and the quiet bureaucracy of building a business. When she doubted herself—“Who am I to bottle Ayurveda?”—he reminded her that intention mattered as much as knowledge, and that she carried both in abundance.

Together, they travelled to small farms and co-operatives, choosing partners the way you choose family—slowly, carefully, with a lot of listening. They met women who still dried herbs on their terraces, men who refused to over harvest, families who believed the soil was borrowed from the next generation. With each handshake, the vision for their little brand grew clearer: if they couldn’t explain every ingredient on the label to their own child one day, it didn’t belong in the jar.

Formulation & workshop photo placeholder

The name Punarvi came the year their daughter was born. Looking at her, they felt the same fierce blend of tenderness and responsibility that they felt for their formulations. They wanted a name that held both rebirth and return—a return to ingredients you recognise, and a rebirth of how we think about beauty. Naming the brand after her was not a marketing choice; it was a quiet vow. If the jars carried her name, they had to be something she would be proud to inherit.

In their home, new batches are still met with the same ritual. She opens the jars with the care of someone lifting a sleeping child. He checks the labels twice, making sure every word is honest, nothing overstated, nothing hidden. Together they test the textures on their own skin first—the under-eyes that have seen too many late nights, the hands that have packed countless orders, the foreheads that bear the weight of bills and dreams. Only when the products feel right to them as parents do they feel ready to send them out into the world.

Family & brand story photo placeholder

Punarvi is not a line built around trends or celebrity faces. It is a line built around evenings when the house is finally quiet, when they sit together and talk about which herbs soothed which customer, which formula needs more patience, which story from a stranger made them cry. Every review, every message from someone who finally found something gentle enough for their skin, is read aloud at the dinner table like a letter from an old friend.

When you take a Punarvi jar into your home, you are not picking up a product that was developed in a boardroom. You are holding something that has been argued over lovingly, tested on the two people who created it, and blessed, silently, by a little girl whose name sits on the label. For this family, Punarvi will always be more than a brand—it is a living promise to show up with the same care, the same honesty, and the same heart that they pour into the child who inspired it.
